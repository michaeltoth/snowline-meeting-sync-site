Why this matters

Acuity intake forms are useful only if the data makes it into Wealthbox cleanly.

Most advisor firms do not need a giant technology overhaul. They need the meeting details to land in the right place every time, without creating duplicates or leaving the team to clean up the CRM after the fact.

Common build scope

  • Map Acuity intake answers into Wealthbox fields or notes
  • Create or update contacts without obvious duplicates
  • Tag contacts by meeting type, source, or service line
  • Create tasks for advisor or operations follow-up
  • Route exceptions when required data is missing

Questions

Common questions about Acuity to Wealthbox

Can I just use a native integration or Zapier template?

Sometimes. The template is usually enough if all you need is a basic event. It gets harder when you need contact matching, household logic, meeting-type tags, workflows, cancel/reschedule handling, or exception alerts.

Do you need admin access?

Usually yes, at least temporarily. I can work with a screenshare or restricted access if your compliance process requires it.

What happens when the automation breaks?

That is what the monthly maintenance is for. I sign up for error alerts, review failures, and fix issues caused by expired connections, changed fields, renamed meeting types, or API quirks.

Can you map Acuity form answers into custom fields?

Yes, when the target field exists and the data format is predictable. For messy or conditional data, I usually document the mapping rules before building.
